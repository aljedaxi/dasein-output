the 0-3 scale

the 0-3 scale is an abuse of Net Promoter Scores. the basic idea is that you have three kinds of customers:

  1. people that love your stuff: your Promoters
  2. people that don't feel any particular way about your stuff.
  3. and people that hate your stuff: your Detractors
because the average rating on google maps is 4 stars, 1/5 to 3/5 are your detractors; 5/5 are your promoters; 4/5 doesn't matter.

i like the system RE: this website because it's about promotion. i made this website because i want to promote some coffee shops (and kinda because i want to hate on some others). NPS is designed to express exactly that: the desire to actively bring up products/companies/pillars of the community.

the act of flattening the standard 1-10 scale into a 1-3 scale is a useful analytical tool if you're someone in marketing that needs a good, single metric. because there's only one of me, it's not super important here.

this ignores that a score is more than a score: it's an index. when Anthony Fantano gives an album you thought was great a 6, there's this understanding that he thought, idk, insert whatever 7/10 album here was better than that thing you liked. a ranking emerges. everything is relative to everything else on the stack.

this is especially relevant when you're me, and you want to help, say, travellers who'll only be able to visit one cafe find the best cafe possible. you need to be able to sort these things, and have the best of the best be visibly skimmable.

(as a teen, i watched a negative review of an anime and understood that i would love it. i did love it. i could never have had an experience like that if the video didn't go into every aspect of the anime, in the same way i would have never heard about aubade if the snob didn't go into every aspect of each cafe. what i like about the format of this site is that we can losslessly surface those little facets of the experience.)
